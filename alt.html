Mamalode

Duration: September 2011 - December 2011

Project Advisors: Julie Kientz, Mike Watt

Design Opportunity

There is a huge scope of opportunity for tablets to revolutionise the magazine reading experience. I worked with General UI, a Seattle based developer, and three other Masters studetns from the Human Centered Design department at the UW to design the v2 of the Mamalode's iPad app. Mamalode is a magazine for mothers which seeks to provide networking and support for mothers.

Process

  • Semi-Structured Interviews: We conducted 5 one hour long interviews regarding their views on motherhood, personal goals, reading habits, and use of technology
  • Social Network Mapping: Asked participants to visually explain their social network and how they are linked
  • Personas: After analysis, three personas (primary, secondary, anti) were created to represent users
  • Ideation: Brainstormed a list of ideas using affinity diagrams
  • Prototyping: Created a paper prototype (for internal use), and a hi-fi prototype using HTML/CSS/jquery to test out our top three ideas from brainstorming
  • Usability Testing: We conducted three test sessions where users interacted with the hi-fi prototype on the iPad. We made some modifications based on the results from these sessions

Results

Based on our findings we incorporated the following design decisions into our hi-fi prototype:

  • Created an immersive reading experience that would focus on magazine content
  • Incorporated at-a-glance features that would support mother's opportunistic reading habits
  • Incorporated relevant sharing features as mothers share information very selectively

Further recommendations have been submitted to General UI, and they are currently working with Mamalode to develop the next phase of the app.

Our prototype may be accessed at http://nkkl.github.com/Mamalode-Prototype/
