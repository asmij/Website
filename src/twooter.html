Mamalode

September 2011 - December 2011

Design Opportunity

There is a huge scope of opportunity for tablets to revolutionize the magazine-reading experience. I worked with General UI, a Seattle-based developer, and three other Masters students from the HCDE department at the UW to design the second version of Mamalode's iPad app. Mamalode is a magazine for mothers which seeks to provide networking and support for mothers.

Process

  • Semi-Structured Interviews: Conducted five one-hour interviews regarding mothers' views on motherhood, reading habits, and use of technology
  • Social Network Mapping: Asked participants to map out their social networks
  • Personas: Developed three personas (primary, secondary, anti) to represent users
  • Ideation: Brainstormed over 200 ideas using affinity diagrams
  • Prototyping: Created a paper prototype (for internal use) and a hi-fi prototype using HTML/CSS/jquery to test out our top three ideas
  • Usability Testing: Conducted three test sessions where users interacted with the hi-fi prototype on the iPad and made modifications according to test results.

Result

Based on our findings we incorporated the following design decisions into our hi-fi prototype:

  • Created an immersive reading experience that would focus on magazine content
  • Incorporated at-a-glance features that would support mothers' opportunistic reading habits
  • Incorporated relevant sharing features as mothers share information very selectively

Further recommendations have been submitted to General UI, and they are currently working with Mamalode to develop the next phase of the app.

Our prototype may be accessed at http://nkkl.github.com/Mamalode-Prototype/
