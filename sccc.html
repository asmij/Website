Seattle Central Community College Website Usability Study

Duration: January 2012 - March 2012

Design Opportunity

Seattle Central Community College (SCCC), located in Capitol Hill, Seattle, will be doing a website redesign in the coming two years. I worked with three other Masters students from the HCDE department at UW to conduct a usability study on the current website. Our goal was to investigate how students were using the website and what design implementations would make the information organization and navigation more usable.

Process

  • Survey and Analysis: Carried out a survey to identify major problem areas and areas of interest on the website.
  • Task Creation: Created tasks related to the previously identified areas to study students' interactions and gain their feedback.
  • Usability Test: Conducted seven test sessions with students who were in their first quarter at SCCC. Sessions consisted of the participants completing the tasks and then a de-brief session and a post-test questionnaire where we gained more insight on how the participants normally used the website. Sessions were recorded using Camtasia.
  • Data Analysis: Analysed data from post-test questionnaire along with metrics like 'task complete success rate', and 'task efficiency'.

Findings

We based our findings on three sources: observing tasks and the results, the post-test questionnaire, and the de-briefing session. We identified 14 findings and grouped them into 4 categories. For each finding, we created a problem statement, and a specific recommendation. In addition, for each problem statement we also also evaluted it according to three dimensions - Scope, Severity, and Complexity.



We provided recommendations for each of these four areas to SCCC. In addition, we also recommended design changes for further usability tests in order to focus on the four identified areas.
