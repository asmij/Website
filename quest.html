QUEST Capstone Project

Duration: August 2010 - December 2010

Project Advisors: Joseph P. Bailey

Design Opportunity

The public and private sectors struggle with creating unified emergency management plans because they lack efficient research, communication and collaboration capabilities. Integrity Consulting identified this opportunity and conceptualized a web platform that will address the needs of the emergency management planning community.

I worked with Integrity Consulting, a Maryland-based consulting firm, and three other undergraduate students from the QUEST Honors program to bring Integrity Consulting's concept of an integrated web platform to life by creating its user interface and documenting its capabilities.

Process

  • Semi-Structured Interviews: Conducted interviews with a few members of EMP to understand how workflow process of the organisation
  • Comparative Analysis: Researched other web platforms that offered services to similar organisation
  • Ideation/Sketches Created sketches of multiple ways to visualize different forms of information archintecture for the web platform. Selected best features after reviewing with client (Integrity Consulting)
  • Prototyping: Created 12 mock ups in PowerPoint for higher level capabilities and presented them to client and made modifications based on feedback

Deliverables

Based on our design sessions we learned some important lessons:

  • Created a set of 12 mock ups to showcase some high level features of the web platform
  • Created a requirements matrix to list out all the features in order or priority that the platform must have
  • Created a process matrix that would apply to different parts of EMP

    • Findings of this project were presented at the QUEST Senior Conference in December 2010. It was also presented to Integrity Consulting and the organization they were working with.
