QUEST Capstone Project

Duration: August 2010 - December 2010

Project Advisors: Joseph P. Bailey

Design Opportunity

The public and private sectors struggle with creating unified emergency management plans because they lack efficient research, communication and collaboration capabilities. Integrity Consulting, a Maryland-based consulting firm, identified this opportunity and conceptualized a web platform that will address the needs of the emergency management planning community.

I worked with Integrity Consulting and three other undergraduate students from the QUEST Honors program to bring Integrity Consulting's concept of an integrated web platform to life by creating its user interface and documenting its capabilities.

Name of organisation changed to EMP for confidentiality reasons.

Process

  • Semi-Structured Interviews: Conducted interviews with members of EMP to understand the workflow process of the organization
  • Comparative Analysis: Researched other web platforms that offered services to similar organizations
  • Ideation/Sketches Created sketches in order to present different forms of information architecture (watch the spelling of that) for the web platform. Selected best features after reviewing with client (Integrity Consulting)
  • Prototyping: Created 12 mock-ups in PowerPoint for high-level capabilities, and made modifications based on feedback from client

Deliverables

Based on our design sessions we learned some important lessons:

  • Created a set of 12 mock-ups to showcase some high level features of the web platform
  • Created a requirements matrix to list all the features the platform must have, in order of priority
  • Created a process matrix that would apply to different parts of EMP

    • Findings of this project were presented at the QUEST Senior Conference in December 2010. It was also presented to Integrity Consulting and the organization they were working with.
