Twooter: Designing a Musical Expression Tool for use in Social Networks

Duration: August 2010 - May 2011

Project Advisors: Greg Walsh, Allison Druin

Design Opportunity

Twooter is a tool for non-verbal, music-based expression in an online social network. Borrowing ideas from popular social networks and influenced through recent educational endeavors by a large cultural institution, Twooter is being designed with the hope of bridging the gap between the ease of updating one's status in a profile and the difficulty in content creation.

I worked on Twooter for my undergraduate research project at the Human-Computer Interaction Lab (HCIL) with Greg Walsh, a PhD student in the iSchool. We co-wrote a paper, based on our reserch and findings, which was accepted to the Interaction Design and Children (IDC) 2011 conference.

Process

  • Copperative Inquiry: Designing children's technologies with children in an intergenerational group names Kidsteam
  • Comparative Analysis: Researched applications that incorporated music visualization and composition. Chose ToneMatrix as a base for our design process
  • Design Sessions: Conducted design sessions with the Kidsteam, which involved design acitivites like "Big Pieces of Paper" to generate ideas and "Sticky Note" technique to gather feedback on the second iteration of the prototype
  • Prototyping: Created hi-fi prototype (based on Twooter) in Adobe Flex. Revised the prototype based on the design session

Findings

Based on our design sessions we learned some important lessons:

  • The app must support visual aspects. Music-only interactions seemed too abstract for design team members to interact with in a production manner.

The app is in its nascent stage and will be continued to be worked upon at the HCIL.
